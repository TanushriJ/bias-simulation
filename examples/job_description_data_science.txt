Junior Data Scientist — Energy Analytics Team

About the Role

As a Junior Data Scientist, you will work within our Energy Analytics team on building data-driven solutions that are essential for the energy transition. Together, we ensure that critical processes around grid optimization, demand forecasting, and anomaly detection run reliably. Our models and pipelines make it possible to extract actionable insights from operational data, contributing to a more efficient and sustainable energy grid.

This is a full-time position (40 hours per week).

What You'll Be Working On

You actively contribute to the development, deployment, and improvement of our machine learning models and data pipelines. You work closely with fellow data scientists, engineers, and business stakeholders.

You will join a diverse team with different levels of experience and specializations, where knowledge sharing and personal growth are central.

As a Junior Data Scientist, you will be:

- Building and validating predictive models for energy demand and grid load forecasting.
- Developing and maintaining ETL pipelines and data infrastructure.
- Collecting requirements from stakeholders and translating them into analytical solutions and user stories.
- Performing exploratory data analysis to uncover patterns in operational data.
- Taking ownership of end-to-end model development — from data collection to production deployment.
- Thinking along about future technical direction and architectural choices.

About Us

We are a leading energy network company managing critical infrastructure for millions of customers. Everything we do is driven by our mission to enable a reliable and sustainable energy future. Our team consists of smart, driven specialists who are passionate about using data to make a real-world impact.

You will work in a Scrum team within our Analytics division, following the SAFe framework. In two-week sprints, you'll collaborate with colleagues and cross-functional teams on quarterly objectives set during PI planning.

The fast-paced environment demands a rockstar problem-solver who can hit the ground running and dominate complex analytical challenges independently.

What We're Looking For

We need someone with a competitive mindset and aggressive drive to deliver results. You bring:

- Master's degree in Data Science, Computer Science, Mathematics, Statistics, or a related field (required).
- Minimum of 1-3 years of hands-on experience with Python and its data science ecosystem (pandas, scikit-learn, TensorFlow/PyTorch).
- Experience with cloud platforms (AWS, Azure, or GCP) and infrastructure-as-code tools.
- Strong SQL skills and experience working with large-scale datasets.
- Familiarity with MLOps practices, CI/CD pipelines, and containerization (Docker, Kubernetes).
- Native-level English communication skills; Dutch is a strong plus.
- Experience at a top-tier tech company or consultancy (FAANG, McKinsey, Deloitte, or similar) is a plus.
- Must be available for occasional evening and weekend deployments.
- DevOps experience is a plus; DevSecOps experience is a strong differentiator.
